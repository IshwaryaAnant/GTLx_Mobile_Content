Walkthrough Problem Part H [Solution]

A block, with a mass of 10kg, is placed on top of an incline, with an angle of 30o, and begins to slip due to the absence of friction. Determine the acceleration at which the block will slide down the slope.



h) Since Fnet in the y-direction is zero, we can conclude that there is no acceleration in that direction. Therefore the only acceleration is in the x-direction, in our case, down the slope. This conclusion matches our intuition of objects sliding down an incline rather than lifting up into the air. Now that we have confirmed the direction and numerical value of the net force on the moving block, we can finally calculate the acceleration of the block sliding down the incline! Try using Newton’s second law to determine this value.


Answer: 4.9m/s2
Newton’s second law tells us Fnet = m*a. The net force only acts in the x-direction. Therefore we can solve for the acceleration.


a = Fnet/mass = 49N/10kg = 4.9m/s2
