Walkthrough Problem Part E [Solution]

A block of mass 2 kg is initially at rest on a flat surface. It has a coefficient of static friction of 0.34 and a coefficient of kinetic friction of 0.25. How much force, F1, is required to get the block moving towards the right?



e. Now, that weâ€™ve calculated the maximum static force that F1 must overcome, which statement below is correct:


A) The F1 must be less than 6.66N for the block to move
B) F1 can be between 0 and 6.66N for the block to move
C) F1 must be greater than 6.66N for the block to move
D) F1 can be greater than or equal to 6.66N for the block to move

Answer: C
The force of static friction acts to keep an object at rest in relation to a surface. In response to any applied force, static friction will produce a corresponding force that is equal in magnitude and opposite in direction. If the applied force exceeds the maximum value of static friction for the given surface, the object will begin to move in the direction of the applied force. In the example above, the maximum force of static friction is 6.66 N. Thus, F1 must exceed this value in order for the block to move along the surface.
