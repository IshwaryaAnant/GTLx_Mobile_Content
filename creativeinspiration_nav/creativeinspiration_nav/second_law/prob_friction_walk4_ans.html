A block of mass 2 kg is initially at rest on a flat surface. It has a coefficient of static friction of 0.34 and a coefficient of kinetic friction of 0.25. How much force, F1, is required to get the block moving towards the right?

Answer: B
Given the expression for maximum static friction, Fsf = Î¼staticFN, the frictional force is simply the product of the coefficient of static friction and the normal force. When an object experiences the maximum amount of static friction, it is because there is an applied force that is about to move the object relative to its point of contact with the frictional surface. If the applied force becomes any greater, then the object will begin to move in the direction of the applied force.
