Problem 1 [Solution]

The same block of mass 10 kg is sliding down a ramp with angle 30°. However, now you are pushing up with a horizontal force of 100 N on the block. What is the acceleration of the block? Will the block slide up or down the ramp?


Answer: 3.76 m/s2
The vector forces are easiest to sum if we take the plane of the incline to be the x-direction. In order to find the acceleration along this plane, we add the various forces acting in this direction. These forces include the x-component of gravity and the x-component of the applied 100 N force. Summing these contributions and setting them equal to ma, we reach the expression
Fcosθ - mgsinθ = 100cos30o - 98sin30o = 10a.
Solving for a, we obtain an acceleration of 3.76 m/s2.
