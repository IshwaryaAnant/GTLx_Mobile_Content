Walkthrough Problem

A block, with a mass of 10kg, is placed on top of an incline, with an angle of 30o, and begins to slip due to the absence of friction. Determine the acceleration at which the block will slide down the slope.



a) First we should think about what causes the block to move down the incline. To do so, we will have to analyze the forces acting on the block. What force(s) do(es) the block experience? Check all boxes that apply.


A) gravitational force
B) normal force
C) tension force from a string
D) frictional force

Answer: A, B
There are two forces acting upon the block: the gravitational force and the normal force. Since we can assume this system exists on Earth, there must be a gravitational force pulling down on the block. There is a normal force acting perpendicular to the incline surface and opposing the component of gravity that is also perpendicular to the incline surface.
