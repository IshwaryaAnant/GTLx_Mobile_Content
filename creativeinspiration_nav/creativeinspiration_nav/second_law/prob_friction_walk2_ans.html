Walkthrough Problem Part B [Solution]

A block of mass 2 kg is initially at rest on a flat surface. It has a coefficient of static friction of 0.34 and a coefficient of kinetic friction of 0.25. How much force, F1, is required to get the block moving towards the right?



To answer this question, we will solve this in parts.


b. Given the force diagram, correctly identify the direction of each force:



Gravitation Force:

A B C D

Normal Force:

A B C D

Frictional Force:

A B C D

Applied Force:

A B C D

Answer: Gravity - 4; Normal - 2; Friction - 1; Applied - 3
As shown in the problem statement diagram, the applied force F1 points to the right. The force of friction always opposes the direction of motion (or the direction that the object would move in the absence of friction), and thus is pointing to the left, opposite F1.
