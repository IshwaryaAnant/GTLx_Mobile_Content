Gravity pulls objects towards the center of the earth, in this case downwards. However we have defined our x and y-axes to be along the slope of the incline. Therefore the gravitational force is split between an x-component pointing downwards along the surface of the incline and a y-component perpendicular and inward to the incline surface.


ii. normal force


A) 1 B) 2 C) 3 D) 4

Answer: A
The normal force is always perpendicular to the contact surface between two objects. As specified in part d, we have have defined the coordinate axes so that the x-axis is parallel to the surface of the incline, while the y-axis is perpendicular to the surface. In this orientation, the normal force lies directly on the positive y-axis. Therefore there is no need to split the normal force into separate components, since all of the force lies in the positive y-direction.
