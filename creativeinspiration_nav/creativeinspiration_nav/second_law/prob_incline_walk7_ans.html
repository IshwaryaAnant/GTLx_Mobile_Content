Walkthrough Problem Part G [Solution]

A block, with a mass of 10kg, is placed on top of an incline, with an angle of 30o, and begins to slip due to the absence of friction. Determine the acceleration at which the block will slide down the slope.



g) Our ultimate goals is to find the acceleration of the block sliding down the incline. We know this sliding motion of the block is caused by the sum of all the forces, known as the net force, acting on the block. Therefore we should find the value of this net force and use Newtonâ€™s second law to determine the acceleration of the block. Remember that we split the forces in the x- and y-components. It will be helpful to continue keeping these components separate. Therefore, first find the sum of all the forces in the x-direction and then in the y-direction. Using the numerical values from part g, calculate:


  1. FNetx, the net force in the x-direction
  2. FNety, the net force in the y-direction

Answer:


  1. FNetx = 49N
  2. FNety = 0N

In the x-direction direction, the only acting force is the x-component of the gravitational force (the normal force has no acting component in the x-direction). Therefore the net force in the x-direction is 49N. In the y-direction we have a positive normal force and the y-component of the gravitational force in the negative direction. Therefore the net force in the y-direction is


FNet,y = Fgy + FN = -89.9N + 89.9N = 0N
