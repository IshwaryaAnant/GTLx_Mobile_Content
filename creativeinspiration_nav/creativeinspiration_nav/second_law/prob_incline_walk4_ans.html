Walkthrough Problem

A block, with a mass of 10kg, is placed on top of an incline, with an angle of 30o, and begins to slip due to the absence of friction. Determine the acceleration at which the block will slide down the slope.



d) Since the block is moving on an incline, it will be helpful to reposition the coordinate axes. How do we choose how to reorient the axes? A good place to start is either lining up the y-axis with the normal force of the object or aligning the x-axis with the direction of acceleration. For this problem, pick the best orientation for the coordinate system:


A B C D

Answer: B
From part c, we determined the block will slide along the incline. Therefore, we should set the x-axis in the same direction along the surface of the incline. In part c, we predicted the block would slide downwards (towards the left), instead of up the incline. Therefore we will set the positive x-direction in the downwards left direction. The y-axis is always set as 90 degrees from the x-axis and it is convention to keep the positive y-direction in the upwards direction.
