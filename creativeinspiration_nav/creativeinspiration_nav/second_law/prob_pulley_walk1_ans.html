Walkthrough Problem

Consider the rope around the massless, frictionless pulley shown below. How much mass must M2 have in order for M1, with mass 14kg, to move upwards at an acceleration of 3.2 m/s2?



a. First, Let us focus our attention on block M1. What forces are acting on this block?


A B C D

Answer: D
The mass M1 is suspended via a rope and is not in contact with any surface. Thus, it experiences only the downward force of gravity and an upward force of tension.


b. According to the problem statement, we want this block to move upwards with an acceleration of 3.2m/s2. Of the forces identified in part a, which force pulls M1 upwards.


A) Gravitational Force
B) Normal Force
C) Frictional Force
D) Tension

Answer: D
Tension is a “pulling” force, thus any rope that is pulling on an object is said to be under tension. This tension is felt by the object as a “pull” of the same magnitude. Thus the tension of the rope holding M1 is exerting the upward force.
